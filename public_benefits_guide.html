Benefits Available to Non-Citizens

Simplified Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)

SNAP is a version of North Carolina’s Food and Nutrition Services program. It is a newly developed initiative that provides food assistance to elderly individuals receiving Supplemental Security Income (SSI). To be eligible for SNAP, individuals must meet the following criteria: they must be 65 years or older, reside in North Carolina, live in a one-person household, and not be living in an institution. Additionally, they must receive SSI and not be enrolled in food and nutrition services in another state.

Work First (WF)

Work First is North Carolina’s Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) program, designed to help families access resources for well-being and economic self-sufficiency. The program is based on the principle that parents are responsible for supporting themselves and their children. It provides parents with short-term training and other services to help them secure employment and achieve financial independence. Employment services are designed to meet the specific needs of each family. Additionally, relatives, grandparents, and legal guardians caring for children can access these services to prevent children from entering the foster care system. General eligibility applies to families with incomes at or below 200% of the federal poverty level. Families can receive benefits for a maximum of 24 months, after which they must wait three years before reapplying for Work First cash assistance. Federal law also enforces a cumulative lifetime limit of 60 months for anyone receiving TANF benefits. It is important to note that North Carolina General Statute 108A-27 grants local county governments discretion in TANF program development. While most counties adhere to the general eligibility guidelines outlined in the Work First manual, some electing counties have additional flexibility in determining eligibility criteria.

Work First Cash Assistance

Work First Cash Assistance is primarily intended for children. Families can apply on behalf of a child, and the approved assistance is included in the family's total payment. There is a state-imposed 24-month limit on receiving cash assistance under TANF, as well as a federal lifetime limit of 60 months.

Supplemental Security Income (SSI)

SSI is a program that provides financial assistance to individuals with limited income and resources who are blind, aged 65 or older, or have a qualifying disability. Unlike other Social Security benefits, SSI payments are not based on prior work history or a family member’s employment record.

State/County Special Assistance (SA)

SA is a North Carolina state supplement to SSI that provides cash benefits to eligible individuals residing in licensed facilities authorized to receive SA payments. To qualify for SA, an individual must be eligible for SSI or be ineligible for SSI solely due to income limits.

North Carolina’s Refugee Services Programs (RSP)

RSPs provide transitional assistance to help refugees and other eligible non-citizens achieve economic self-sufficiency and successfully integrate into their communities. These programs are funded by the U.S. Office of Refugee Resettlement (ORR) and are designed to offer refugee-specific services approved under federal regulations. Local agencies receive this funding to deliver various support services. Programs include the following:

  • Refugee Support Services (RSS): RSS assists refugees with resettlement and helps them achieve economic self-sufficiency. Services include employment assistance and English language training. RSS is combined with Reception and Placement Services (R&P) and is delivered by local resettlement agencies to newly arrived refugees.
  • Refugee School Impact (RSI) / Early School Impact: The RSI program provides funding for activities that support the integration and education of eligible children up to age 18.
  • Services to Older Refugees (SOR): SOR provides funding for programs that promote integration and wellness for refugees aged 60 and older. These services help eligible individuals access aging-related support in their communities and develop social connections to reduce isolation.
  • Refugee Youth Mentoring (YM): This program is designed for eligible youth aged 15-24, pairing them with an adult mentor to support their educational and vocational advancement. It focuses on developing social and life skills, cultural integration, health and financial literacy, and career and educational development.
  • Refugee Health Promotion (RHP) & Refugee Mental Health Initiative (ReMHI): RHP provides funding to promote refugee health by improving health literacy, coordinating healthcare services, and offering wellness groups. ReMHI helps ORR-eligible persons within their first five years of eligibility, helping with urgent or underserved mental health needs.

Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC)

WIC provides nutrition services to improve health outcomes for pregnant and postpartum women, infants, and children up to age five. The program offers free healthy foods, nutrition education, breastfeeding support, and referrals to other services.

Refugee Cash Assistance (RCA)

RCA provides financial support to refugees who do not qualify for Work First Cash Assistance, Supplemental Security Income (SSI), or the Matching Grant program. It is specifically intended for eligible individuals without dependent children. This short-term program is available for up to 12 months from the refugee’s arrival date, helping them transition during resettlement.

Refugee Medical Assistance (RMA)

The RMA program offers healthcare coverage to refugees who need medical care but do not qualify for other health programs. Like RCA, it is a short-term program available only during the first 12 months after a refugee’s arrival.

North Carolina Medicaid

Medicaid in North Carolina provides coverage for a wide range of health services, including doctor visits, check-ups, emergency care, dental care, vision and hearing services, prescription drugs, maternity care, hospital services, behavioral health, wellness services, and medical-related devices. NC Medicaid is available at little to no cost to recipients, with no monthly premiums. Copayments, where applicable, will not exceed $4 for certain services. Applying for Medicaid does not affect immigration status or an individual’s chances of becoming a permanent resident or U.S. citizen.

Food and Nutrition Services (FNS)

FNS is a federal food assistance program that helps low-income families afford nutritious foods. In North Carolina, FNS benefits are distributed through an Electronic Benefits Transfer (EBT) card, which functions similarly to a debit or credit card. The EBT card can be used to purchase food at most grocery stores across the state, with funds loaded onto the card each month.

Subsidized Child Care Assistance (SCCA)

SCCA provides financial assistance for childcare to families who demonstrate a need and meet eligibility requirements. The program is funded through a combination of state and federal resources to help ensure access to affordable childcare services.

In-State Tuition and North Carolina State Financial Aid

Legal residents of North Carolina who have maintained at least 12 months of uninterrupted domicile may qualify for in-state tuition. The state partially subsidizes tuition costs at North Carolina public colleges and universities for eligible students.

Federal Student Aid (FSA), Grants, and Loans

The FSA program provides financial assistance through grants, work-study opportunities, and loans to help eligible students and families pay for college or career school.

Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI)

SSDI provides monthly payments to individuals who are unable to work due to a disability and have worked for at least five of the last ten years before becoming disabled. Eligibility and payment amounts are based on the individual’s work history before the disability began.

Child Support Programs (CSS)

CSS services assist parents and guardians of minor children with child support matters. These services include establishing child support orders, enforcing existing orders, collecting and processing child support payments, and determining paternity.

Low Income Energy Assistance Program (LIEAP)

LIEAP provides a one-time annual payment to energy providers to help eligible families cover heating expenses. This federally funded program is available to qualifying households in North Carolina.

Crisis Intervention Program (CIP)

CIP is a federally funded program that assists households facing heating or cooling-related crises. Applicants must present a final notice or past-due bill and demonstrate a health-related or life-threatening emergency to qualify for crisis funds. To be eligible, at least one member of the household must be a U.S. citizen or a non-citizen who meets eligibility criteria.

Office of Refugee Resettlement (ORR) Refugee Resettlement Program Benefits & Services

ORR eligible populations may receive benefits and resettlement services upon their arrival. This includes time-limited cash and medical assistance, case management services, English as a Foreign Language classes, job readiness and employment services.

Federal Housing Programs

Federal housing programs provide funding for various housing assistance initiatives. These include direct aid for low-income renters, grants and assistance for states, localities, and nonprofits to address housing needs, and loans and loan guarantees to support homeownership.
